Matchday 5: League Phase Race Tightens

Matchday 5 of the 2025–26 Champions League league phase is packed with heavyweight clashes. Chelsea host Barcelona, Arsenal face Bayern München, Manchester City take on Leverkusen, and Olympiacos welcome Real Madrid in a huge night that could reshape the top of the table. With only a few games left, every point matters for clubs chasing a top-eight finish and direct qualification to the round of 16.

Focus: standings pressure, knockout play-off race, and which contenders look strongest heading into the final matchdays.

How the New 36-Team League Format Works

The 2025–26 season introduces the new Champions League format: one 36-team league table where each club plays eight league-phase games against different opponents. The top eight teams qualify directly for the round of 16, while clubs placed 9th–24th enter two-legged knockout play-offs to claim the remaining spots.

The expanded format means more big-match combinations, more drama in the middle of the table, and extra pressure on consistency across all eight matchdays.

Puskás Aréna: Stage for the 2026 Champions League Final

The road to glory will end at the Puskás Aréna in Budapest, host of the 2026 Champions League Final. With a capacity of around 67,000 and a modern bowl design, the stadium offers an impressive backdrop for European football’s biggest night.

As the league phase unfolds, every club dreams of making it to Budapest and lifting the trophy under the lights.
